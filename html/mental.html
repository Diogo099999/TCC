Anxiety and Depression Crisis: What It Is and Symptoms UNDERSTAND IN SUMMARY

An anxiety crisis, also known as a panic attack or panic crisis, is a sudden episode of intense fear or discomfort that peaks quickly and is accompanied by a series of physical and emotional symptoms. These crises can occur unexpectedly and are often very frightening, even though they do not represent a real danger.

Symptoms of an Anxiety Crisis:

  • Palpitations or increased heart rate
  • Shortness of breath or difficulty breathing
  • Excessive sweating
  • Tremors or shaking
  • Feeling of choking
  • Chest pain or discomfort
  • Nausea or abdominal discomfort
  • Dizziness, vertigo, or feeling faint

These symptoms can last from a few minutes to about half an hour, but the emotional impact can last much longer.

During an Anxiety Crisis: Steps to Alleviate Symptoms

  1. Controlled Breathing: Breathe slowly and deeply: Inhale through your nose counting to four, hold your breath for four seconds, and then exhale slowly through your mouth counting to four. This helps calm the nervous system.
  2. Focus on Something Physical: Hold an object and focus on its details. This helps divert attention from anxious thoughts.
  3. Practice Mindfulness: Observe what is happening around you without judgment, focusing on sounds, colors, or physical sensations.
  4. Positive Affirmations: Repeat reassuring phrases like "This will pass" or "I am safe."
  5. Move: Simple movements like walking or stretching help dissipate anxious energy and reduce tension.

What Is a Depressive Crisis?

A depressive crisis is an intense period of worsening depression symptoms. During this crisis, a person may feel deep sadness, hopelessness, lack of energy, and loss of interest in activities that were once pleasurable. These feelings may be accompanied by intense negative thoughts, and in severe cases, suicidal thoughts may arise.
