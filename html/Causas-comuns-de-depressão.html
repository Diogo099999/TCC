Common Causes of Depression

Explore some of the most frequent contributing factors.

1. Genetic Factors

Individuals with a family history of depression may have a higher risk of developing the condition themselves. Studies suggest that genetics can influence the way our brains respond to stress and manage mood-regulating chemicals.

2. Chronic Stress and Trauma

Long-term exposure to stressful situations, such as work pressure, family conflict, or past trauma, can lead to depression. Chronic stress affects the brain’s structure and chemistry, potentially making it harder to regulate emotions effectively.

3. Chemical Imbalances in the Brain

Depression is often linked to imbalances in neurotransmitters, which are chemicals that help control mood and emotion. These imbalances can make it challenging for individuals to manage stress and may lead to depressive symptoms over time.

Understanding the causes of depression can help in finding the right approach to manage and treat it. Consult a mental health professional for more guidance.
